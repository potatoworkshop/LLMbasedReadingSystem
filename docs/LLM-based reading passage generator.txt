LLM-based reading passage generator

===== PAGE 2 =====

Motivation
Static English reading materials
• Slow updates
• Discrete difficulty levels (A2/B1/B2…)
• Difficulty in personalizing and adapting to learner abilities
Could LLM serve as a "dynamic reading material engine" to automatically generate 
and adjust the difficulty of reading texts for reading comprehension training?

===== PAGE 3 =====

Research Questions
• Can LLM generate stable reading articles based on specified topics, lengths, and levels?
• Input parameter: topic, target words, model
• How can "language difficulty" be converted into a calculable metric?
• Using quantitative evaluation with classic readability metrics like Flesch Reading Ease.
• Can LLM rewriting (simplify / complexify) reflect differences in metrics?
• How to build a complete pipeline?

===== PAGE 4 =====

Improvement
• Automated reading passage generation with OpenAI’s large language model (Ummugul Bezirhan)
• Limitation: It used an old model, lacked quantifiable and computationally readable methods, and employed human evaluation.
• Free-text Rationale Generation under Readability Level Control (Yi-Sheng Hsu)
• Limitation: It merely tested the readability of different models in explaining the problem, lacking readability control.
• Generating Educational Materials with Different Levels of Readability using LLMs (Huang)
• Limitation: Using descriptions to generate articles results in information distortion and uneven distribution of difficulty modifications.
Points for improvement that can be generally found in relevant research
The difficulty metrics have been increased:
• Information density, the ratio of different word counts to total word count, 
• Ratio of keywords by using TF-IDF
• Average abstraction level, based on the concreteness ratings database.
